Flipkart is an Indian e-commerce marketplace founded in 2007 by Sachin and Binny Bansal. It started as an online bookstore and has since expanded into a wide range of categories, offering over 80 million products. Flipkart is a major player in the Indian e-commerce market, competing with global leaders like Amazon. Key aspects of Flipkart: Founding and Origins: Founded in 2007 by Sachin Bansal and Binny Bansal, Flipkart started as an online bookstore. E-commerce Marketplace: Flipkart offers a diverse range of products across various categories. Operational Scale: Flipkart has a large customer base (over 500 million), delivering across India and having numerous sellers on its platform. Acquisition by Walmart: In 2018, Walmart acquired a majority stake in Flipkart. Growth and Innovation: Flipkart has a history of innovation, pioneering services like Cash on Delivery and No Cost EMI. Corporate Culture: Flipkart emphasizes a people-centric culture, with values like Audacity, Bias for Action, Customer-First, Integrity, and Inclusion. Future Plans: Flipkart is committed to transitioning to electric vehicles by 2030 and has been actively investing in R&D.
